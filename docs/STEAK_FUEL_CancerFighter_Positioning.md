## THE SCIENCE BEHIND WHY THIS WORKS

*The research validates what Renji experienced firsthand.*

### 1. The Protein Crisis in Cancer Treatment

Cancer cachexia — the progressive muscle and weight loss that accompanies many cancer treatments — is not simply about eating less. It is a metabolic shift driven by tumour activity, inflammation, and treatment side effects. The medical literature is clear: patients on chemotherapy require **a minimum of 1.4g of protein per kilogram of body weight daily** just to maintain muscle mass. Lower than that, and muscle wasting accelerates — weakening the patient, impairing treatment tolerance, and extending recovery.

For a 70kg person, that's 98g of protein per day. From real food. While nauseous.

STEAK FUEL delivers 67g of complete protein per 100g. A single 30g portion — the size of four strips — delivers more than 20g of protein. That is clinically significant protein in a portion that fits in a palm, requires no preparation, no refrigeration, and can be eaten in three minutes between rest periods.

### 2. Why Air-Cured Meat Specifically

The oncology nutrition literature consistently recommends **soft, tender, easily chewable protein** for patients with ileostomies and those experiencing FOLFOX-related mouth sores and fatigue. Air-cured biltong is:

- Tender (not tough or fibrous like jerky)
- Pre-portioned naturally into small strips
- Shelf-stable (no refrigeration — critical during hospital stays and travel to treatment centres)
- Free of nitrites and artificial preservatives (important for immunocompromised patients)
- Rich in Heme-Iron and Vitamin B12 — nutrients that support red blood cell production during chemotherapy-induced anaemia

### 3. The Ileostomy Advantage

For patients who have undergone total or partial colectomy with stoma formation, the nutritional challenge is compounded. Without a large intestine, water and nutrients move rapidly through the remaining small intestine. Absorption window is shorter. Concentration matters more than volume.

This is precisely where caloric and protein density becomes a clinical asset:
- A 30g serving of STEAK FUEL delivers ~20g protein with no dilutants, fillers, or empty carbohydrates
- The portion size is appropriate for small, frequent feeding (5–6 times per day, as recommended for ileostomy patients)
- The texture is soft enough to chew thoroughly — essential for maximising absorption through a shortened GI tract

### 4. The Iron Snack Stack — Omega-3 + Protein in One Sitting

Research supports the combination of **high protein + omega-3 fatty acids + BCAAs** as the most effective nutritional intervention for cancer-related muscle preservation. The IRON SNACK 7-seed cracker provides:

| Seed | Primary Benefit |
|---|---|
| Chia | Omega-3 ALA, hydration support |
| Flaxseed | Anti-inflammatory lignans, Omega-3 |
| Hemp | Complete protein, all essential amino acids |
| Pumpkin | Zinc, magnesium — immune support |
| Sunflower | Vitamin E, selenium — antioxidant |
| Sesame | Calcium, iron |
| Watermelon | L-citrulline — reduces fatigue |

Paired with STEAK FUEL beef, mutton, or chicken — and a small portion of full-fat cheese for caloric density — this is a recovery stack that fits in a snack bag.
